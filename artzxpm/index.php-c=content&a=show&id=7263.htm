  Zhao Mengfu (flu) (October 20, 1254 - July 30, 1322), Ziang, Han, Songxue Dao, Crystal Palace Dao, Gulbo, was appointed by Meng Fu in middle age. A native of Wuxing, Zhejiang Province. From the end of Southern Song Dynasty to the beginning of Yuan Dynasty, he was a famous calligrapher, painter and poet. He was the grandson of Zhao Kuangxu XI and the descendant of Zhao Defang, king of Qin Dynasty.
  In the 23rd year of the Yuan Dynasty (1286), Zhao Mengfu was recommended by Shi Chengjufu, who served on the platform. He was honored by Kublai Khan, the ancestor of the Yuan Dynasty. He had successively held the posts of Jixian Bachelor, Governor of Jinan Road, Confucianism Promotion of Jiangsu and Zhejiang Provinces, and Hanlin Bachelor of Service. Dr. Ronglu, a Bachelor of Arts in Hanlin, a retired official. In his later years, he gradually retired and begged for help from illness. In 1322, Zhao Mengfu died in 69. He was donated to Pingzhang and Wei Guogong in Zhongshu Province of Jiangsu and Zhejiang Province as Wenmin, so he was called Zhao Wenmin. His works include The Collection of Songxue Zhai Literature and so on.
Zhao Mengfu is knowledgeable and versatile. He is good at poetry and writing, knows economy, calligraphy, fine painting, is good at gold and stone, general law and Lu, and interprets and appreciates. Especially in calligraphy and painting achievement is the highest. In painting, he pioneered a new style of painting in the Yuan Dynasty, known as the "crown of the Yuan Dynasty". Zhao Mengfu was also famous for his good seal, official script, true script, line script and cursive script, especially regular script and line script. His style of writing is beautiful, elegant, well-organized and well-written. He created Zhao Ti book, which is also known as the "four masters of regular script" together with Ouyang Xun, Yan Zhenqing and Liu Gongquan.
