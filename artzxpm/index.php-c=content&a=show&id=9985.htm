 Chang Dai-Chien, male, from Neijiang, Sichuan Province, was born in Panyu, Guangdong Province, on May 10, 1899 in Anliangli, the suburb of Neijiang City, Sichuan Province. He was a Chinese ink-splashing painter and calligrapher.
In the 1950s, Zhang Daqian traveled around the world and gained great international reputation. He was praised by the western art circles as the "Oriental Brush". [1]
  He and his second brother Zhang Shanzi Kunzhong founded the "Great Wind Hall School", which is the most legendary ink-splashing painter in the 20th century Chinese painting circle. Especially in landscape painting has made great achievements. After living abroad, painting style and writing combined, heavy color, ink and wash merged into one, especially splashing ink and splashing color, creating a new artistic style, because its poems, books, paintings and Qi Baishi, Pu Xinshe, it is also known as "Nanzhang Beiqi" and "Nanzhang Beipu", many names like Niu Mao. With Huang Junbi and Pu Xinshe, they are known as "three families crossing the sea". In his twenties, he grew a big beard, which became a special symbol of Zhang Daqian's future.
